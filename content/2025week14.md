## News Summary

**Rivalry Week Delivers Mixed Results for ACC**

Week 14 featured several traditional rivalry games with significant implications for ACC teams and their postseason positioning. The results showcased both the conference's strengths and vulnerabilities heading into the final stretch of the regular season.

**Major Upsets and Surprises**

California pulled off a stunning 38-35 victory over #21 SMU in a thrilling upset that could significantly impact College Football Playoff considerations. The loss dealt a major blow to SMU's postseason hopes. Duke dominated Wake Forest 49-32 in an offensive showcase, highlighting the Blue Devils' impressive late-season surge and offensive capabilities.

**Dominant Performances**

#12 Miami (FL) delivered a statement 38-7 victory over #22 Pittsburgh, maintaining their strong positioning for postseason play and demonstrating their defensive prowess. Louisville shutout Kentucky 41-0 in the Battle for the Governor's Cup, showcasing complete dominance on both sides of the ball. #9 Notre Dame continued their impressive campaign with a 49-20 rout of Stanford, solidifying their credentials as a top-10 program.

**Rivalry Game Outcomes**

The Commonwealth Cup saw #18 Virginia decisively defeat Virginia Tech 27-7 in a convincing display. NC State dominated North Carolina 42-19 in their traditional rivalry matchup, while Clemson secured a 28-14 victory over South Carolina, maintaining bragging rights in the Palmetto State. Boston College defeated Syracuse 34-12 in a solid performance on the road.

**Concerning Losses**

#23 Georgia Tech fell to unranked #4 Georgia 16-9 in a defensive struggle that highlighted the Yellow Jackets' challenges against top-tier competition. Florida State suffered a disappointing 40-21 loss to Florida, continuing their difficult season and raising questions about the program's trajectory.

## Commentary Summary

**Conference Standings and Playoff Implications**

The Week 14 results significantly impacted ACC standings and College Football Playoff positioning. Miami's dominant victory over Pittsburgh solidified their case for playoff consideration, while SMU's upset loss to California dealt a major blow to their postseason aspirations. Georgia Tech's loss to Georgia, despite being ranked, underscores the competitive challenges ACC teams face against elite SEC programs.

**Team Performance Analysis**

Several ACC teams demonstrated marked improvement in their final regular season games. Duke's explosive 49-point performance against Wake Forest showcased the Blue Devils' offensive potential and late-season momentum. Meanwhile, Louisville's shutout victory over Kentucky highlighted their defensive capabilities and coaching preparation, setting a strong tone heading into bowl season. Virginia's dominant win over Virginia Tech further established the Cavaliers as one of the conference's most consistent performers.

**Looking Ahead**

With the regular season concluding, ACC teams are now focused on bowl preparations and potential playoff scenarios. Miami appears best positioned for a major bowl game or playoff consideration, while several other ACC teams have secured bowl eligibility with their Week 14 victories. The conference will look to build on these performances in postseason play.

**Broader ACC Trends**

Week 14 revealed both the strength and vulnerability of the ACC. While teams like Miami, Virginia, and Duke delivered impressive performances, the losses by ranked teams like Georgia Tech and SMU highlighted the competitive challenges facing ACC programs against quality opponents. The mixed results in rivalry games suggest the conference possesses notable depth while also raising questions about overall strength compared to other Power Five conferences.

The week ultimately reinforced that while the ACC has several strong programs capable of competing at the highest level, consistency across the conference remains a challenge as teams prepare for postseason play. Success in bowl games will be critical for the conference's overall perception heading into the offseason.
